## Design Context

### Users
Patients who want to understand and take control of their health data. The app serves four distinct personas:
- **Preventive Performer** — proactive, data-driven, wants optimization
- **Concerned Preventer** — anxious, wants reassurance and clarity
- **Digital Optimizer** — tech-savvy, expects a polished digital experience
- **Clinic-Anchored Loyalist** — traditional, trusts their doctor, needs simplicity

Usage context varies by persona: daily check-ins, weekly analysis, or post-appointment review.

### Brand Personality
**Precise. Trustworthy. Clinical.**

The interface must instill trust and a sense of control. Patients are dealing with their health data — the design must feel authoritative without being cold, and empowering without being overwhelming.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Medical/Clean** — inspired by premium medical devices and clinical dashboards, not consumer wellness apps.

- Clean, structured layouts with generous whitespace
- Muted, sophisticated palette — no neon, no gradients-for-gradients-sake
- Typography-driven hierarchy — data should be scannable and digestible
- Dark theme as primary, but refined — not "gamer dark" or "AI dark"
- Precision in every detail: alignment, spacing, data presentation

**Anti-references**: Random, chaotic, or overly playful designs. No generic SaaS dashboards. No wellness-app pastel aesthetics.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over decoration** — every element serves a purpose. No ornamental flourishes.
2. **Data speaks first** — biomarkers, scores, and trends are the hero. UI gets out of the way.
3. **Progressive disclosure** — simple by default, detailed on demand. Adapts to persona complexity needs.
4. **Quiet confidence** — the interface should feel like a trusted medical instrument: precise, reliable, calm.
5. **Hierarchy through typography** — use type weight, size, and spacing to create structure, not borders and boxes.
